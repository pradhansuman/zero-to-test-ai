Chapter 1: Rational Numbers

Key Concepts

  • Expressed as p/q where q ≠ 0; p, q are integers
  • All integers are rational (e.g., 5 = 5/1)
  • Includes terminating and repeating decimals
  • Closed under +, −, ×, ÷ (excluding ÷ by 0)
  • Additive inverse of p/q is −p/q; multiplicative inverse is q/p
  • Rational numbers are dense — infinite rationals between any two

Chapter 2: Linear Equations in One Variable

  • Cross-multiplication for equations with fractions

Chapter 3: Understanding Quadrilaterals

Key Concepts

  • Sum of interior angles of any quadrilateral = 360°
  • A polygon with 4 sides, 4 vertices, 4 angles, 2 diagonals
  • Exterior angle sum of any convex polygon = 360°
  • Parallelogram: opposite sides parallel & equal
  • Rhombus: all sides equal; Rectangle: all angles 90°

Chapter 4: Practical Geometry

Key Concepts

  • A quadrilateral is uniquely constructed with 5 measurements
  • 5 measurements may include sides, diagonals, or angles
  • Special case: square needs only 1 side; rectangle needs 2 sides
  • Diagonal method: divide quadrilateral into 2 triangles (SAS/SSS)

Step-by-Step: Parallelogram ABCD (AB=5cm, AD=3cm, ∠A=60°)

1Draw base AB = 5 cm with a ruler.
2At point A, use a protractor to draw ∠DAB = 60°.
3With compass at A, set radius 3 cm. Mark point D on the 60° ray.
4With compass at B (radius = AD = 3 cm), draw an arc.
5With compass at D (radius = AB = 5 cm), draw an arc intersecting step-4 arc. Label intersection C.
6Join BC and DC. ABCD is the required parallelogram.

Solved Examples

Example: Construct square PQRS with side 4 cm
Step 1: Draw PQ = 4 cm
Step 2: At P, construct 90°. Mark PS = 4 cm
Step 3: At Q, draw arc 4 cm. At S, draw arc 4 cm. Intersection = R
∴ PQRS is the required square (all sides 4 cm, all angles 90°)

Chapter 6: Squares and Square Roots

Key Concepts

  • A perfect square is the product of a number with itself: n² = n × n
  • Numbers ending in 2, 3, 7, 8 are never perfect squares
  • √n by prime factorisation: pair up prime factors
  • Pythagorean triplet: (a, b, c) where a² + b² = c²

Chapter 9: Algebraic Expressions and Identities

Key Concepts

  • Monomial: 1 term; Binomial: 2 terms; Polynomial: many terms
  • (a+b)² = a² + 2ab + b²
  • (a−b)² = a² − 2ab + b²
  • (a+b)(a−b) = a² − b²
  • Multiplication: distribute each term of one polynomial over other

Chapter 10: Visualising Solid Shapes

Key Concepts

  • 3D shapes have Faces (F), Edges (E), Vertices (V)

Chapter 11: Mensuration

Key Concepts

  • Area of trapezium = ½ × (a+b) × h
  • Area of circle = πr²; Circumference = 2πr
  • Volume of cuboid = l × b × h
  • Volume of cylinder = πr²h; Lateral surface area = 2πrh
  • Area of rhombus = ½ × d₁ × d₂

Chapter 13: Direct and Inverse Proportions

  • Inverse proportion: x × y = constant (k); x ↑ ⟹ y ↓
